# Biblical Analytics

The analytics dashboard provides four heatmap visualizations across all 66 books of the Bible.

## Cross-Reference Density

A 66 x 66 matrix where each cell shows how many cross-references exist between two books. White lines mark the OT/NT boundary.

**What to look for:**

- Bright diagonal = books referencing themselves
- Bright off-diagonal blocks = books that heavily reference each other
- The Isaiah row is particularly bright — the most cross-referenced OT book
- Romans and Hebrews light up across many OT books

## Topic Distribution

66 books x top 25 Nave's Topical Bible themes. Shows which books discuss which themes most.

**What to look for:**

- Psalms dominates many topic columns (it covers nearly every biblical theme)
- "Jesus Christ" topics concentrate in the Gospels and Epistles
- "Law" topics cluster in the Pentateuch
- Some themes bridge OT and NT — shared theological concepts

## Ethics Landscape

66 books x 5 ethical reasoning dimensions, based on AI-generated chapter classifications:

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Commonsense** | Everyday moral intuition |
| **Deontology** | Duty and obligation reasoning |
| **Justice** | Fairness and equity |
| **Virtue** | Character and moral excellence |
| **Utilitarianism** | Consequences and welfare |

!!! note
    Only 288 chapters are classified. Books without classified chapters appear as black rows.

**What to look for:**

- Proverbs and Psalms score high on virtue ethics
- Leviticus and Deuteronomy emphasize deontological (rule-based) reasoning
- The Prophets lean toward justice
- The Epistles balance across multiple dimensions

## Word Frequency

66 books x top 30 most-used Hebrew/Greek words (Strong's numbers). Gold columns are Hebrew words, blue are Greek.

**What to look for:**

- Some Hebrew words appear *only* in the OT (top-left quadrant)
- Greek words appear *only* in the NT (bottom-right quadrant)
- A few concepts bridge both testaments through their linguistic roots
- The most common words reveal each book's core vocabulary
