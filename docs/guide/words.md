# Word Study

The Word Study view displays 14,298 Strong's Concordance entries — every Hebrew and Greek word in the Bible — as a 3D point cloud clustered by semantic similarity.

## The Two Clusters

You'll see two distinct masses:

- **Hebrew** (warm gold, left) — 8,674 Old Testament words
- **Greek** (cool blue, right) — 5,624 New Testament words

They're separated because the AI embeddings encode the *definitions* from different scholarly lexicons (BDB for Hebrew, Thayer for Greek). Within each cluster, semantically related words are near each other.

## Point Size

Larger points = more frequently used words. A massive sphere is a word that appears hundreds of times across scripture. Tiny dots are rare, specialized terms.

## Clicking a Word

Click any point to open the Word Detail panel showing:

- Original Hebrew/Greek characters
- Transliteration and pronunciation
- Root definition
- Full BDB/Thayer lexicon entry (expandable)
- KJV translation variants
- Sample verses showing the word in context

## Filtering

Toggle **OT** to see only Hebrew words, **NT** for only Greek. This lets you explore one language's semantic landscape in isolation.

## Floating Labels

Semi-transparent "Hebrew" and "Greek" labels float above each cluster for orientation.
